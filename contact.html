The Bohemian poet and writer Maxwell Bodenheim participated in a debate with play writer Ben Hecht. The debate was about a curious and provocative resolution: "That people who attend literary debates are imbeciles."
Hetch went first, speaking in favor of the resolution. Arguing cogently from the lectern, he spoke with great eloquence. After a rousing performance, he concluded with a flourish, "The affirmative rests."
Hetch sat down and Bodenheim woked up to the lectern. He scrutinized the audience for several moments without saying a word. He then looked over at Hetch and said:

You win.
